Frequently Asked Questions

Do you want to know where to start or which certification is best for your goals? Our FAQs cover the basics, such as how to sign up, how the courses are structured, what you need to do to pass the PMP exam, and how to study in a way that works for you.

What makes your project management classes in New Zealand unique?

We combine global project management standards with knowledge of the local industry in our courses. Every class is taught by certified trainers who have worked on real projects, so you learn both practical skills and recognised qualifications.

Do I need to have done project management before I can start a course?

You don’t need any prior experience to get into entry-level programmes like CAPM. To get your PMP certification, you’ll need to have worked on relevant projects. Our team can help you check this before you sign up.

How long does it take to get a PMP certification in New Zealand?

Most people who are getting ready for the PMP exam do so in 8 to 12 weeks, depending on how fast they study and what their schedule is like. We have flexible options, like weekend and online classes, to meet the needs of professionals in New Zealand.

Do people around the world know about your project management classes?

Yes. Employers all over the world recognise our PMP and CAPM certifications, which are based on the PMBOK Guide. When you finish your training here in New Zealand, you will have credentials that can be used anywhere in the world.

Is it possible to take your project management courses NZ online?

Of course. We offer both in-person and online training, so you can learn anywhere in New Zealand without missing out on interaction or help from your teacher.

How do I sign up for a course to get a project management certification?

You can choose your preferred certification path—PMP, CAPM, or PMI-CP—by calling our admissions team or filling out the online form. We will help you sign up and make a study schedule.

Get in touch with our team to help you find the best course and schedule for your career goals.
